## Investment Philosophy

### Core Principle
**"Buy the Dip, Hold for Wealth"** - Deploy lump sum capital only during market corrections to maximize long-term returns while managing downside risk.

### When to Invest: Same Day vs Next Day?

| Scenario | Recommendation | Reason |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| Market falls 3-5% in a day | **Next Trading Day** | Allow volatility to settle, avoid catching a falling knife |
| Market falls gradually over weeks | **Same Day** | Correction is already priced in |
| Global event triggers fall | **Wait 2-3 Days** | Events need time to fully reflect |
| India-specific correction | **Same Day** | Local corrections recover faster |

> **Pro Tip:** For mutual funds, place orders before 1:30 PM to get same-day NAV. For ETFs, buy during market hours when spread is tight (11 AM - 2 PM is optimal).

   - Platforms: Groww, Zerodha Coin, Kuvera, MF Utilities

   - Rebalance if any category exceeds Â±5% of target allocation
   - Book profits if any fund grows >40% of original investment

4. **Maintain Emergency Fund Separately**
   - Keep 6-12 months expenses in FD/Liquid funds
   - Never invest emergency money in equity

5. **Document All Gifts**
   - Keep gift deeds for money transferred to parents/HUF
   - Maintain paper trail for tax scrutiny

### âŒ DON'Ts

1. **Don't Try to Time the Bottom**
   - You'll never catch the exact bottom
   - Stick to the layer-based deployment strategy

2. **Don't Exit in Panic**
   - Market recoveries are swift and sharp
   - Missing the best 10 days can halve your returns

3. **Don't Over-Diversify**
   - 5-7 funds are enough
   - Too many funds = index-like returns with higher costs

4. **Don't Chase Past Returns**
   - Last year's topper is often this year's laggard
   - Focus on consistency and fund house reputation

5. **Don't Ignore Expense Ratios**
   - 1% extra expense = 10-15% less corpus over 10 years
   - Prefer Index funds/ETFs for large cap allocation
