## 12. Risk Analysis & Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Low footfall initially** | High | High | Strong pre-launch marketing, aggregator presence, offers |
| **High staff turnover** | High | Medium | Above-market pay, good culture, growth path |
| **Rising food costs** | Medium | High | Multiple suppliers, menu engineering, seasonal menus |
| **Negative reviews** | Medium | High | Quality control, fast response, service training |
| **Cash flow crunch** | Medium | High | 6-month working capital reserve, monitor daily |
| **Regulatory issues** | Low | High | Complete all licenses before opening, hire CA |
| **Equipment breakdown** | Medium | Medium | AMC contracts, backup for critical equipment |
| **New competitor opens** | Medium | Medium | Strong brand loyalty, unique menu, community |
| **Pandemic / lockdown** | Low | Very High | Delivery-ready model, cloud kitchen pivot capability |

## Key Success Factors for Tier B Cities

1. **Price Sensitivity Matters** — Keep prices 20–30% lower than metro restaurants for similar quality
2. **Taste is King** — In cities like Indore, people are extremely food-conscious. Taste > Ambiance > Price
3. **Consistency** — Same taste every single time. This makes or breaks restaurants
4. **Local Palate Adaptation** — Even if serving global cuisine, adapt to local taste preferences (spice levels, sweetness)
5. **Owner Presence** — In Tier B cities, owner-operated restaurants build trust faster
6. **Word of Mouth** — Stronger than any paid marketing. One viral dish can make your restaurant
7. **Delivery Game** — 30–50% of revenue can come from delivery. Don't treat it as secondary
8. **Community Building** — Become a neighbourhood favourite, not just another restaurant
9. **Cost Control** — Monitor food cost daily, not monthly. Small leaks sink ships
10. **Patience** — Expect 6–12 months to stabilize. Don't panic in month 2
